The Return of Gradients: Why "Flat Design" is Dying

For a decade, the web was flat. Solid colors, sharp edges, and minimalism ruled. But look at Stripe, Apple, or Vercel today—gradients are back, but they are different this time.

Modern vs. Retro Gradients

In the early 2000s, gradients were harsh and metallic. Today's gradients are subtle, often used to create a "glow" effect or to guide the user's eye across the screen. They add depth without adding clutter.
